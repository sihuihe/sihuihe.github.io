Single lens camera

Design a camera with F/16 and EFL = 80mm, total FOV = 53 degree, flat image surface, wavelength = 0.4, 0.44, 0.49, 0.56, 0.68, reference wavelength = 0.49um

As it is a single lens, a large F# is needed to get a good image quality

1st step:

Assume aperture is at the lens. This can help to reduce lateral color and distortion. Optimize the lens with EFFL = 80 to ensure the right focal length, and the thickness of glass < 1mm to prevent the non-reasonable thick lens. Then I get

From the 3D layout, we can obviously find the Petzval field curvature aberration.Look at the ray fan:

As there's little aberration at 0 degree, it means little spherical aberration.

The linear shape of aberration at large angle looks like defocus increases with angle, which is due to the Petzval field curvation aberration, consistent with what we find on the layout.

The different aberration at x and y direction shows astigmatism.

Coma is U shape. It is not obvious here.

Distortion only causes lateral shift. It is hard to observe from there.

The spot diagram shows large spot size at off-axis

2nd step:

To correct the aberration, one way is to make the incident angle smaller. We can add another variable -- the position of the stop.

Stop in front of the positive lens lends to barrel distortion

Stop after the positive lens leads to pincushion distortion, which is more subjectively disturbing as the picture looks like flying apart. So we add the stop in the front.

Off-axis: Tangential field (Y axis) shows u shape, which indicates coma

Sagittal field (x axis): field curvature (defocus increases with angle)
