Three-lens camera

Design a camera with F/3.5 and EFL (effective focal length) = 52mm, total FOV = 45.2 degree, flat image surface, wavelength = 0.45, 0.5, 0.55, 0.6, 0.65, reference wavelength = 0.55um

As it contains three lenses, a smaller F# (3.5) is possible to get a good image quality.

1st step:

Choice of glasses: To correct chromatic dispersion, use crown and flint glasses with +ve and -ve lenses. To reduce field curvature and generally other aberrations, use high index glasses. Therefore, we choose crown glass LaFN 21(n=1.788, V=47.5) and flint glass SF53(n=1.728, V=28.7), which are among Schott's preferred glass types.

First order optics: As we need a positive system power, and a symmetric design about the stop can help reduce coma, lateral color and distortion, we choose the three lenses to be positive, negative, and positive, respectively. A simple 1st order layout is to have the positive lens with EFL = 52mm, negative lens with EFL = -52mm, and the third lens with the same power as the first one. To simplify the calculation, let the 1st and 3rd lens to be plano-convex lens with flat surface inside, the two surfaces of the 2nd lens have the same curvature but opposite signs.

The focal lens of a thin lens is given by

$\frac{1}{f}=(n-1)*[\frac{1}{R_1}-\frac{1}{R_2}]$
